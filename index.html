Samgyetang Season

Jjimjilbang Weather

Snow on Palaces

December snow on Gyeongbokgung rooftops is the most photogenic version of Seoul. ~7 snowy days average.

3 NIGHTS

Signiel Seoul

Floors 76–101 of Lotte World Tower (555m). 235 rooms in the sky. Two Michelin-starred restaurants on-site.

“Sleeping above the 76th floor of the world’s 5th tallest building. The altitude IS the experience.”

Day 1 • Saturday
Dec 20: Arrival & Kowloon-Level Street Food
Afternoon

Arrive Incheon → Check Into the Sky

Private car to Signiel Seoul (~1 hr). Your room starts above the 76th floor of a 555-meter tower. Floor-to-ceiling windows. The entire city below you.

Only here
★ No NYC hotel puts you at 300+ meters altitude
5:00 PM

Cheonggyecheon Stream Winter Walk

11km illuminated urban stream cutting through downtown. December light installations transform it into a glowing winter corridor. Then walk to Myeongdong.

Culture
6:30 PM

Myeongdong Street Food Baptism

Tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes — Seoul’s soul food), hotteok (sweet syrup pancakes, perfect in freezing air), bungeoppang (fish-shaped pastry with red bean). The entire alley steams in the December cold.

Food
★ Street food in freezing air with steam rising from every cart
Late Night

Pojangmacha Tent Bar

Soju + pajeon (scallion pancake) at an orange plastic tent bar. THE Korean winter night experience. Order sundae (blood sausage — nothing like European versions) and odeng (fish cake skewers in broth).

FoodOnly here
Day 2 • Sunday
Dec 21: Palace Snow, Secret Garden & Gwangjang Feast
7:30 AM — Breakfast

Tosokchon Samgyetang

5 Jahamun-ro 5-gil. Since 1983. Ginseng-stuffed whole chicken in milky-white broth enriched with pine nuts, walnuts, black sesame. Heated floors, wooden beams. THE winter Seoul breakfast — next door to Gyeongbokgung.

Breakfast
★ Samgyetang on heated floors in a freezing Seoul morning
9:30 AM

Gyeongbokgung Palace + Hanbok

14th-century Joseon Dynasty palace. Rent hanbok (traditional dress) — free palace entry + incredible photos. Guard-changing ceremony at 10 AM. If it has snowed: the rooftops dusted white are breathtaking.

Culture
★ Two brothers in hanbok at a 14th-century palace. Peak Korea.
11:00 AM

Bukchon Hanok Village

600-year-old traditional houses on the hillside between two palaces. Walk the 8 scenic viewpoints. Paparazzi-free in winter.

Culture
12:30 PM — The Main Event

Gwangjang Market

Korea’s first permanent market (1905). Bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes, crispy-edged) + makgeolli (rice wine). Mayak gimbap (“addictive rice rolls”). Yukhoe (Korean beef tartare, silky-fresh). Sundae (blood sausage). Each vendor has specialized in one dish for decades.

Food
★ Single-dish specialists perfecting one item for 40+ years
2:30 PM

Changdeokgung Secret Garden (UNESCO)

Guided tour only — book online immediately (sells out in minutes). 78 acres of pavilions, frozen ponds, ancient trees. Winter bare branches over ice. Nearly empty in December. The most atmospheric palace in Korea.

UNESCO
★ Secret royal garden in winter silence
4:30 PM

Insadong Tea House Warm-Up

Traditional Korean tea (ssanghwa-cha herbal, omija-cha five-flavor berry) in a hanok tearoom. Defrost after 6 hours of outdoor exploring.

Snack
7:00 PM — Dinner

Korean BBQ at Maple Tree House

Premium hanwoo beef grilled tableside. This is Korean BBQ in its homeland — a different universe from NYC. Order galbi, chadolbaegi, and let the servers handle the grill.

Food
★ Hanwoo beef graded at a level that doesn’t exist outside Korea
Day 3 • Monday
Dec 22: Ancient Temple, Korean Spa & Mingles Farewell
7:00 AM — Breakfast

Namdaemun Market

Korea’s oldest market (1414). Kalguksu (hand-cut noodle soup — boiling broth, chewy noodles, winter perfection). Vendors who’ve run the same stall for 40+ years. Open since 5 AM.

Breakfast
9:30 AM

Bongeunsa Temple

1,200-year-old Buddhist temple nestled beneath Gangnam skyscrapers. 23m Maitreya Buddha statue. Winter incense in freezing air. The juxtaposition of ancient temple and modern city is extraordinary.

Temple
★ 1,200-year-old temple at the foot of Seoul’s glass towers
11:00 AM

Ikseon-dong

Seoul’s oldest hanok neighborhood, now the coolest — tiny cafes, galleries, and vintage shops in 100-year-old alleyways. Grab tteokbokki from a street vendor.

Culture
12:30 PM

Jokbal Alley (Jangchung-dong)

An entire alley of braised pig’s trotters restaurants. Rich, gelatinous, impossible at this level anywhere else. Then: chimaek (fried chicken + beer) at a nearby joint. Korea’s national pastime.

Food
2:00 PM

Siloam Sauna — Jjimjilbang

Multi-floor Korean bathhouse near Seoul Station. Jade rooms, salt rooms, sleeping halls, egg-head towels. More local than Dragon Hill, open 24/7. THE cultural experience of Korea — especially in freezing December.

Only here
★ Multi-floor bathhouse culture does not exist in America
4:30 PM — Jjimjilbang Snack

Sauna Eggs & Sikhye

Inside the jjimjilbang: maekbanseok gyeran (eggs baked on hot stones until the whites turn brown and nutty) and sikhye (sweet fermented rice punch, ice cold). Wrap your head in the iconic sheep-towel and snack on the heated floor. This is peak Korean comfort.

Snack
★ Stone-baked eggs in a bathhouse. A Korean ritual.
7:30 PM — Farewell Dinner

Mingles ⭐⭐⭐ (3 Michelin Stars)

Chef Mingoo Kang’s modern Korean. Asia’s 50 Best Restaurants. Seasonal Korean ingredients reimagined. No NYC outpost — this restaurant exists only in Seoul.

Fine Dining
★ No. 13 in the world. Exists only in Seoul. Book 4 weeks ahead.

Hanoi

Dec 23 (arrive) – Dec 25 (drive to bay) • 2 Nights • Old Quarter, Temples & Street Food
2 NIGHTS

Capella Hanoi

47 rooms. 1920s opera-house theme. Bill Bensley design. Each room a different theatrical character. French Quarter.

“The most stylish hotel in Hanoi, by the world’s most celebrated tropical architect.”

Day 4 • Tuesday
Dec 23: Arrival & Old Quarter After Dark
Midday

Arrive Hanoi → Capella

Private car from Nội Bài Airport (~45 min). Check into Capella Hanoi. 47 rooms designed by Bill Bensley. 1920s opera-house theme — each room a different character.

Luxury
3:00 PM

Old Quarter & Tran Quoc Pagoda

36 ancient streets each named for its original trade. Walk to Hoàn Kiếm Lake. Cross the red bridge to Ngọc Sơn Temple. Then taxi to Tran Quoc Pagoda — Hanoi’s oldest (6th century), sitting on a tiny island in West Lake. Sunset over the water with the pagoda silhouetted. The nature-y temple highlight of Hanoi.

Temple
★ 6th-century pagoda on an island at sunset over West Lake
5:00 PM

Cà Phê Giảng — The Original Egg Coffee

39 Nguyễn Hữu Huân. Invented 1946 by a bartender from the Sofitel Metropole (where you’ll stay later). Beaten egg yolk, condensed milk, strong drip coffee. Upstairs at tiny wooden tables overlooking the alley.

Iconic
★ Egg coffee at the place that invented it. 1946. Same family.
7:00 PM — Dinner

Bún Chả Đắc Kim

1 Hàng Mành. Charcoal-grilled pork patties with rice noodles, herbs, and dipping broth. Smoky, sweet, herbaceous. The dish that put Hanoi on the global food map.

Food
★ Charcoal smoke billowing from the street grill. Impossible in NYC.
Late Night

Bia Hơi Corner

Fresh draft beer — 25 cents a glass — on tiny plastic stools at the intersection of Tạ Hiện and Lương Ngọc Quyến. The Old Quarter’s nightly street party. Order bún dậu mắm tôm (fried tofu with fermented shrimp paste) from a cart — polarizing, unforgettable.

FoodOnly here
Day 5 • Wednesday
Dec 24: Temple of Literature, Water Puppets & Christmas Eve
7:00 AM — Breakfast

Phở Thìn Bổ Hồ

19 Hàng Vôi (NEW address — old location demolished for metro construction). THE legendary phở. Since 1979. Beef phở with quẩy (fried dough sticks). The broth has been perfected for 45+ years. Tiny shop. Enormous flavor.

Breakfast
★ 45-year-old phở broth at the source. Not a replica.
9:00 AM

Temple of Literature

Vietnam’s first university (1070). Five courtyards, ancient banyan trees, stone stelae on turtle backs listing 1,000 years of scholars. The most serene place in Hanoi.

Temple
★ A 956-year-old university with stone turtles and banyan trees
10:30 AM — Snack

Bánh Mì 25 + Xôi Yên

Bánh Mì 25 (25 Hàng Cá): perfect bánh mì — crisp baguette, pâté, pork, pickled carrot. Then Xôi Yên (35B Nguyễn Hữu Huân): 40-year sticky rice institution with chicken, pork floss, fried shallots.

Snack
11:30 AM

Thăng Long Water Puppet Theatre

800-year-old Vietnamese tradition — puppets perform ON water. Live traditional orchestra with drums and bamboo flutes. Originated in Red River Delta rice paddies. Book the 11:30 or 6:30 show.

Culture
★ Water puppetry: 800 years old, exists only in Vietnam
2:00 PM

Hòa Lò Prison (“Hanoi Hilton”)

French colonial prison turned wartime POW facility. Senator McCain was held here. Sobering, fascinating history.

Culture
3:30 PM — Afternoon Snack

Bún Bò Nam Bộ + Fresh Fruit

Bún Bò Nam Bộ at the famous stall on 67 Hàng Điểu — Hanoi’s signature dry beef noodle with peanuts, fried shallots, and herbs. Light enough for a snack, punchy enough to remember. Then grab a cup of fresh-cut mango and dragon fruit from any Old Quarter fruit cart — December produce is excellent.

Snack
★ Hanoi’s signature dry noodle. Essential dish, impossible to skip.
7:00 PM — Christmas Eve Dinner

Gia Hanoi

Chef Sam Tran’s Michelin-starred modern Hanoian. Elevated local cuisine with seasonal ingredients. Intimate setting. Christmas Eve special menu in a restored colonial space.

Fine Dining
★ Michelin-starred Hanoian cuisine. No NYC equivalent exists.
11:00 PM — Christmas Eve

St. Joseph’s Cathedral Midnight Mass

French Gothic (1886). Thousands of locals gather — not as tourists, but as celebrants. The square fills with vendors, lights, energy. Arrive by 10:30 PM. Vietnamese Christmas is joyful, chaotic, beautiful.

SeasonalOnce a year
★ Vietnamese Catholic Christmas energy in a French Gothic cathedral

Day 7 • Friday
Dec 26: Tai Chi at Sunrise → Yen Tu Sacred Mountain
6:00 AM

Tai Chi at Sunrise on the Cruise

Final morning on the Ylang. Sunrise over 1,600 limestone karsts rising from mist. The bay is perfectly still at dawn. This is the image you’ll remember forever.

★ Tai chi on a deck surrounded by prehistoric limestone towers at dawn
7:30 AM

Cooking Class & Final Cave Visit

Vietnamese cooking class on board — fresh spring rolls and phở from scratch. Then a quick visit to a floating pearl farm or Sung Sốt Cave depending on tide.

Food
9:30 AM

Disembark → Drive to Yen Tu Mountain

Private car from Tuan Chau Harbor to Yen Tu Mountain (~1.5 hrs, 70 km west). The most sacred Buddhist mountain in Vietnam — founded in 1299 when King Trần Nhân Tông renounced his throne after defeating the Mongols and became a Buddhist monk on this peak. He founded the Trúc Lâm Zen sect, the only purely Vietnamese school of Buddhism. Ancient cedars, granite stairways, mountain mist.

SacredNature
11:30 AM — Vegetarian Lunch

Temple Cuisine at Yen Tu Base

Buddhist vegetarian lunch at a base-of-mountain restaurant: xôi ngỗ (sticky rice with five-color toppings), nấm kho tộ (caramelized mushrooms in claypot), canh measure sen (lotus seed soup), and măng tre luộc (bamboo shoots). Eaten by monks for centuries. Mostly grown on the mountain itself.

Snack
★ Buddhist temple cuisine grown on a sacred Vietnamese mountain
1:00 PM — The Climb

Hoang Long Cable Car → Hoa Yen Pagoda

First cable car (8 min) lifts you to Hoa Yen Pagoda — the founding pagoda of Vietnamese Zen Buddhism. 700-year-old yew trees, weathered red lacquer, incense thick in the cold mountain air. Walk the stone path between ancient stupas.

Temple
2:30 PM — The Summit

Bach Long Cable Car → Dong Pagoda (1,068m)

Second cable car (12 min) climbs to An Kỳ Sinh, then a 30-minute walk up granite steps to Dong Pagoda — the bronze pagoda at the summit. Cast in 2007, weighs 70 tons. Panoramic views over northeastern Vietnam reach to the Bai Tu Long Bay coast on clear December days. The most sacred Buddhist site in Vietnam.

NaturePilgrimage
★ A bronze pagoda at 1,068m, the founding site of Vietnamese Buddhism
4:30 PM

Drive Back to Ha Long City

Cable cars down. Private car back to Ha Long city (~1.5 hrs). Check into A La Carte Ha Long Bay ~6:30 PM. Suite with floor-to-ceiling bay views.

Bay views
7:30 PM — Dinner

Cha Muc & Banh Cuon Halong

Take a 10-min taxi to Co Ngu or Bahari Bay (Halong’s top local restaurants). Order chả mực — pounded squid cake fried golden, the Halong dish that exists nowhere else. Plus bánh cuốn chả mực (steamed rice rolls served with squid cake instead of pork) — the local riff. Fresh oysters and grilled sea snails.

Food
★ Cha muc — Halong's signature pounded squid cake. Local-only.
9:30 PM — Nightcap

A La Carte 25th-Floor Sky Bar

Back at the hotel. Cocktails at the rooftop bar overlooking the entire bay at night. The Sun World Queen Cable Car (world's largest cabins, 230-person capacity, tallest cable car pillar at 188.88m) glows in the distance — you can see it spanning the bay from your seat.

Dec 27 (arrive) – Dec 28 (drive to Hanoi) • 1 Night • Mega-Temples & Karst Boats

Tam Cốc Garden Resort

15 bungalows in rice paddies at the base of limestone karsts. Wake up surrounded by the landscape. 5-minute walk to the boat pier.

“Not a resort that overlooks nature — a resort INSIDE the nature.”

Day 8 • Saturday
Dec 27: Bay-View Sunrise & Asia’s Largest Temple
7:00 AM

Sunrise Swim → A La Carte Breakfast

Optional 25th-floor sunrise swim — the bay turns gold from your infinity-pool perch. Then the famous A La Carte breakfast (legitimately one of the best hotel breakfasts in Vietnam: pho noodles made-to-order, banh cuon, fresh fruit, French pastries, banh mi station).

Breakfast
9:30 AM

Drive Ha Long → Ninh Bình

Private car (~3.5 hrs through the Red River Delta). Highway scenery: rice paddies, water buffalo, conical-hat farmers, scattered limestone karsts. Arrive ~1 PM.

1:00 PM — Roadside Snack

Cơm Cháy Ninh Bình (Scorched Rice)

Stop at a local restaurant on arrival. Cơm cháy — Ninh Bình’s signature dish: crispy scorched rice topped with stir-fried goat, mushrooms, or shrimp in a sizzling sauce. The rice crust shatters when you bite it. Only made properly in this province. Pair with nem cốm (green rice spring rolls).

Snack
★ Scorched rice is Ninh Bình’s signature — not found elsewhere
2:30 PM — The Temple Highlight

Bài Đính Temple Complex

Southeast Asia’s largest Buddhist temple. 500 La Hán (Arhat) stone statues lining the path up the mountain — each 2.5m tall, each unique. 36-ton bronze Buddha. 100-ton bronze bell. Multi-tiered pagodas against dramatic karst mountains. This is the temple your brother will remember for the rest of his life.

TempleOnly here
★ 500 unique Arhat statues lining a mountain path. Nothing like this anywhere.
5:00 PM

Check into Tam Cốc Garden

15 bungalows literally IN the rice paddies. Karst towers rising on all sides. Watch sunset from your bungalow as the landscape turns golden.

Unique Stay
7:00 PM — Dinner

Ninh Bình Mountain Goat

The local specialty. Mountain goat prepared 7 ways: grilled, steamed, stir-fried, hotpot, spring rolls, salad, congee. Eaten nowhere else with this quality.

Food
★ Mountain goat seven ways — a Ninh Bình-only tradition
Day 9 • Sunday
Dec 28: Sunrise Boat Through Caves → Metropole
6:30 AM

Tam Cốc Sunrise Boat Ride

5-minute walk from your bungalow to the pier. Rowboat through three limestone caves, rice paddies on both sides, karst towers rising into mist. Called “Ha Long Bay on land.” At sunrise, no crowds, perfect light, silent except for the oars.

Nature
★ Rowboat through prehistoric caves at sunrise. The intimacy is unreal.
9:00 AM

Tràng An Scenic Landscape (UNESCO)

Boat through cave-temples and limestone grottoes. Film location for Kong: Skull Island. You enter caves by boat and emerge into hidden valleys with shrines. Choose Route 1 (9 caves, 3 temples, 2.5 hrs).

UNESCONature
11:30 AM — Post-Boat Snack

Ninh Bình Pineapple & Sugarcane Juice

Back at the pier, grab fresh-pressed sugarcane juice (nước mía) from a roadside vendor — ice cold, crushingly sweet, $0.50. Plus bánh té (Ninh Bình’s sticky rice cake wrapped in banana leaf, filled with pork and mung bean). Fuel for the drive.

Snack
12:00 PM

Drive to Hanoi → Sofitel Metropole

Private car (~2.5 hrs). Check into Sofitel Legend Metropole ~3 PM. THE grand dame of Hanoi since 1901. Charlie Chaplin, Graham Greene, Jane Fonda.

4:00 PM

Metropole War Bunker Tour

Underground bunker discovered in 2011 during renovation. Built 1964 as a bomb shelter. Led by a hotel historian with wartime artifacts. Free for guests, 5 PM and 6 PM daily. The Metropole’s bartender invented egg coffee at Cafe Giang — the circle closes.

Culture
★ Vietnam War bunker beneath a French colonial hotel. Discovered by accident.
5:30 PM — Afternoon Snack

Metropole Le Club Bar & Egg Coffee #2

Settle into Le Club — the Metropole’s legendary bar. Order a cà phê trứng (egg coffee) here for the full-circle moment: the bartender who invented egg coffee at Cafe Giang in 1946 was a Metropole employee. Then: bánh gối (crispy fried pillow cakes filled with pork and glass noodle) from a nearby street vendor. Hanoi’s best afternoon snack.

Snack
7:30 PM — Farewell Dinner

Chả Cá Lã Vọng

14 Chả Cá Street. Vietnam’s most famous dish — turmeric-marinated fish sizzled tableside with dill and spring onion. The restaurant is SO famous the entire street was renamed after it. Operating since 1871. Five generations.

Food
★ The street was literally renamed after this restaurant. Since 1871.
1 NIGHT

Sofitel Legend Metropole
