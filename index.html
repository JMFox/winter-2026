Seoul

Dec 19 (Sat) – Dec 23 (Wed AM) · 4 Nights · Palaces, Temples, Markets
4 NIGHTS

Signiel Seoul

Floors 76–101 of Lotte World Tower (555m, world’s 5th tallest). 235 rooms in the sky. Two Michelin-starred restaurants on-site (STAY by Yannick Alleno + BICENA).

“The altitude IS the experience. No NYC hotel puts you 300m above the city.”

Day 1 · Saturday Dec 19
Arrive Seoul — Stream Lights & Myeongdong Steam
Afternoon

Arrive Incheon → Check Into the Sky

Private car to Signiel Seoul (~1 hr). Your room starts above the 76th floor of a 555m tower. Floor-to-ceiling windows. Entire city below.

Only here
★ No NYC hotel puts you at 300+ meters altitude
5:00 PM

Cheonggyecheon Stream Winter Walk

11km illuminated urban stream cutting through downtown. December lights transform it into a glowing winter corridor. Walk to Myeongdong.

Culture
6:30 PM

Myeongdong Street Food Baptism

Tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes — Seoul’s soul food), hotteok (sweet syrup pancakes, perfect in freezing air), bungeoppang (fish-shaped pastry with red bean). The entire alley steams in the December cold.

Food
★ Steam rising from carts in -5°C air. NYC has no equivalent.
Late night

Pojangmacha Tent Bar

Soju + pajeon (scallion pancake) at an orange plastic tent bar. THE Korean winter night experience. Order sundae (blood sausage) and odeng (fish cake skewers in broth).

FoodOnly here
Day 2 · Sunday Dec 20
Palaces in Snow & Gwangjang Feast
7:30 AM — Breakfast

Tosokchon Samgyetang

5 Jahamun-ro 5-gil. Since 1983. Ginseng-stuffed chicken in milky broth with pine nuts, walnuts, black sesame. Heated floors, wooden beams. Next door to Gyeongbokgung.

Breakfast
★ Samgyetang on heated floors in a freezing Seoul morning
9:30 AM

Gyeongbokgung Palace + Hanbok Rental

14th-century Joseon Dynasty palace. Rent hanbok (traditional dress) — free palace entry + incredible photos. Guard ceremony 10 AM. Snow on rooftops if lucky.

Culture
★ Two brothers in hanbok at a 14th-century palace. Peak Korea.
11:00 AM

Bukchon Hanok Village

600-year-old traditional houses on the hillside between two palaces. Walk the 8 scenic viewpoints.

12:30 PM — The Main Event

Gwangjang Market

Korea’s first permanent market (1905). Bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes) + makgeolli (rice wine). Mayak gimbap (“addictive rice rolls”). Yukhoe (Korean beef tartare). Sundae (blood sausage). Each vendor specializes in one dish for 40+ years.

Food
★ Single-dish specialists perfecting one item for decades
2:30 PM

Changdeokgung Secret Garden (UNESCO)

Guided tour only — book online immediately (sells out in minutes). 78 acres of pavilions, frozen ponds, ancient trees. Winter bare branches over ice. Nearly empty in December.

UNESCO
★ Secret royal garden in winter silence
4:30 PM

Insadong Tea House Warm-Up

Traditional Korean tea (ssanghwa-cha, omija-cha) in a hanok tearoom. Defrost after 6 hours of outdoor exploring.

Snack
7:00 PM — Dinner

Korean BBQ at Maple Tree House

Premium hanwoo beef grilled tableside. Korean BBQ in its homeland.

Food
★ Hanwoo beef graded at a level that doesn’t exist outside Korea
Day 3 · Monday Dec 21
Ancient Temple, Korean Spa & Chimaek Night
7:00 AM — Breakfast

Namdaemun Market

Korea’s oldest market (1414). Kalguksu (hand-cut noodle soup) — boiling broth, chewy noodles. Vendors who’ve run the same stall for 40+ years. Open since 5 AM.

Breakfast
9:30 AM

Bongeunsa Temple

1,200-year-old Buddhist temple beneath Gangnam skyscrapers. 23m Maitreya Buddha. Winter incense in freezing air. Ancient temple at the foot of glass towers.

Temple
★ 1,200-year-old temple framed by Seoul’s tallest skyscrapers
11:00 AM

Ikseon-dong

Seoul’s oldest hanok neighborhood, now the coolest — tiny cafes, galleries, vintage shops in 100-year-old alleyways. Grab tteokbokki from a street vendor.

12:30 PM

Jokbal Alley (Jangchung-dong)

Entire alley of braised pig’s trotters restaurants. Rich, gelatinous, impossible at this level anywhere else.

Food
2:00 PM

Siloam Sauna — Jjimjilbang

Multi-floor Korean bathhouse near Seoul Station. Jade rooms, salt rooms, sleeping halls, egg-head towels. More local than Dragon Hill, open 24/7. THE cultural experience of Korea.

Only here
★ Multi-floor bathhouse culture does not exist in America
4:30 PM — Jjimjilbang Snack

Sauna Eggs & Sikhye

Inside the jjimjilbang: maekbanseok gyeran (eggs baked on hot stones until brown and nutty) and sikhye (sweet fermented rice punch, ice cold). Wrap your head in the sheep-towel and snack on the heated floor.

Snack
7:30 PM — Chimaek Night

Korean Fried Chicken + Beer Crawl in Hongdae

Chimaek = chicken + maekju (beer). Korea’s national pastime. Hit Yoogane for dakgalbi or Kkanbu Chicken for double-fried bone-in. Then bar-hop the Hongdae alleys.

FoodOnly here
★ Double-fried Korean chicken with pickled radish at the source
Day 4 · Tuesday Dec 22
Suwon UNESCO Day Trip & Mingles Farewell
9:00 AM

KTX to Suwon

30-minute KTX from Seoul Station. Arrive at the doorstep of an 18th-century UNESCO city.

10:00 AM

Hwaseong Haenggung Palace

King Jeongjo’s 1796 royal residence inside the fortress walls. Smaller and more intimate than Gyeongbokgung. Watch the changing-of-the-guard ceremony.

Culture
11:30 AM

Hwaseong Fortress Walls (UNESCO)

Walk 5.7 km of 18th-century stone fortress walls (gentle, mostly flat with stairs at the gates). 4 grand gates, command posts, beacon mounds. UNESCO 1997.

UNESCO
★ Walking 230-year-old fortress walls in winter mist
1:30 PM — Lunch at Suwon’s Most Iconic Spot

Yeonpo Galbi — The Original Korean BBQ Rib (Since 1945)

King Jeongjo’s royal kitchen perfected galbi here in the 1790s. Yeonpo has been serving the canonical Suwon-style version since 1945. This is the actual origin of Korean BBQ as you know it.

Food
★ Korean BBQ at the literal birthplace of the dish
3:00 PM

Korean Folk Village (Yongin)

30-min taxi from Suwon. Living open-air Joseon-era village with 240 traditional houses. Scheduled performances throughout the day — equestrian feats, tightrope walking, wedding ceremony reenactment, farmers’ music. Real artisans demonstrating weaving, pottery, blacksmithing.

Culture
★ Joseon-era village frozen in time, with live performances
5:30 PM

KTX Back to Seoul

Quick reset at Signiel before the farewell dinner.

7:30 PM — Farewell Dinner

Mingles ⭐⭐⭐ (3 Michelin Stars)

Chef Mingoo Kang’s modern Korean. Asia’s 50 Best Restaurants top 15. Seasonal Korean ingredients reimagined. No NYC outpost — this restaurant exists only in Seoul.

Fine Dining
★ 3-Michelin Korean fine dining. Exists only in Seoul.

Osaka

Dec 23 (Wed) – Dec 25 (Fri AM) · 2 Nights · Kuidaore (Eat-Til-You-Drop)

Zentis Osaka

Boutique design hotel by Tara Bernerd — Scandinavian calm fused with Japanese craft (Shigaraki-yaki bedside tables from Shiga, bento-box modular room layouts). 212 rooms, opened 2020. Design Hotels member. Michelin-starred chef Shinya Otsuchihashi runs the in-house French-Japanese restaurant Upstairz.

“The most genuinely cool design hotel in Osaka — Japandi craft, not corporate-luxury. Plus Michelin in-house dining means a great Day 5 arrival meal.”

Hozenji Yokocho Lantern Alley

Culture
★ Lantern-lit Edo-era stone alley in modern Osaka

Snack

Kushikatsu Daruma (Since 1929)

The original kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers, panko-crusted) shop. NEVER DOUBLE-DIP the communal sauce — the cardinal sin. 40+ skewer varieties: pork, lotus root, eggplant, scallop, mochi, banana.

Food
★ The original 1929 kushikatsu joint, communal sauce, no double-dipping

Dotonbori Glico-Sign Walk

The iconic Osaka neon strip. The Glico Running Man. Crab signs, blowfish lanterns, river reflection. Best at night when every sign is lit.

Iconic
Day 6 · Thursday Dec 24 · CHRISTMAS EVE
Kuromon Market & Castle Christmas Lights
8:00 AM — Breakfast

Kuromon Ichiba Market

“Osaka’s kitchen.” 580m of stalls, 170+ vendors. Fresh uni shells (cracked open in front of you), seared scallops, A5 wagyu skewers grilled over binchotan, fugu (blowfish), live snow crab tanks.

Breakfast
★ Uni straight from the shell. Live snow crab. Wagyu skewers.
11:00 AM

Osaka Castle + Park

Toyotomi Hideyoshi’s 1583 fortress (current concrete reconstruction is 1931). The 8-floor castle museum is fine; the real draw is the moat, walls, plum garden, and atmosphere of the Park. Walk the outer moat.

Culture
1:30 PM — Lunch

Mizuno Okonomiyaki (Michelin Bib Gourmand, Since 1945)

Tiny shop in Dotonbori. The cult Osaka okonomiyaki spot. Cooked at your table on the iron griddle. Yamaimo (mountain yam) added for fluffy texture — Mizuno’s signature. Order the “Yamaimoyaki.”

Food
4:00 PM — Snack

Umeda Sky Building Christmas Market

German-themed Christmas market beneath the iconic 1993 Hiroshi Hara skyscraper. Mulled wine, sausages, ornaments. Open Christmas Eve — the final day before market closes Dec 25. Then take the elevator to the open-air Floating Garden Observatory, 173m up.

Christmas
★ German Christmas market under a Japanese skyscraper
7:30 PM — Christmas Eve Dinner

Sushi Hara (Edo-mae omakase)

Top Osaka sushi counter. 10-seat counter, 18-course omakase. Premium tuna, uni, anago. ~¥25,000-40,000/pp. Book 3+ weeks ahead.

Fine Dining
10:00 PM — Nightcap

Bar Augusta Tarogawa — Speakeasy

Hidden Osaka cocktail bar, decades-old, ice carved by hand. Christmas Eve nightcap.

Kinosaki Onsen

Dec 25 (Christmas Day) · 1 Night · 7-Bath Yukata Walk & Snow Crab Kaiseki
1 NIGHT

Nishimuraya Honkan

Since 1861. 35 rooms, traditional sukiya-zukuri architecture. Member of Relais & Châteaux. On the willow-lined canal in the heart of Kinosaki. Multi-course crab kaiseki + Tajima beef.

“Christmas Day at a 165-year-old ryokan. Snow crab brought to your table. Wooden corridors, tatami, gas-lamp evening walks.”

Day 7 · Friday Dec 25 · CHRISTMAS DAY
Snow Crab Christmas at an 1861 Ryokan
9:00 AM

JR Kinosaki Limited Express — Osaka to Kinosaki

2.5-hour scenic ride through Hyogo countryside. Direct, no transfers. The train approaches Kinosaki along a small river lined with willows.

12:00 PM

Arrive Kinosaki & Check In

Walk 5 minutes from station to Nishimuraya Honkan. Kimono-clad staff greet you with welcome tea and seasonal sweets. Change into yukata (cotton robe) and geta (wooden clogs) provided by the ryokan.

Luxury
1:30 PM — Lunch

Daikichi — Crab Donburi

Light lunch at a local kiosk before the kaiseki feast. Snow crab over rice, hand-shredded by the chef. Tajima beef croquette on the side.

Snack
★ Snow crab fresh off the boat from the Sea of Japan
2:30 PM

7-Bath Yukata Walk Begins

Guests get free passes to all 7 public bathhouses (sotoyu). Walk between them in yukata + geta on snowy stone bridges. Each bath has a different theme:

· Satonoyu (largest, with rotenburo outdoor bath) · Goshonoyu (most beautiful, garden setting) · Ichinoyu (cave bath) · Mandara-yu (cylinder cypress tubs) · Yanagi-yu (smallest, willow tree views) · Jizo-yu (with Jizo Buddha statue) · Kounoyu (the oldest, founded in the 6th century by a stork)

Onsen
★ Walking between 7 ancient public baths in winter yukata + clogs
7:00 PM — Christmas Dinner

Snow Crab Kaiseki + Tajima Beef

Multi-course kaiseki served in your tatami room. Whole snow crab presented live, then prepared 5+ ways: sashimi, grilled in shell, hotpot (kani-suki), tempura, rice gruel finish (zosui). Plus Tajima beef shabu-shabu — the original wagyu, before Kobe and Matsusaka cattle were bred from this stock. Sake from the Tajima region.

Fine Dining
★ Whole snow crab kaiseki on Christmas Day at an 1861 ryokan. Possibly the trip’s peak food moment.
9:30 PM — Final Bath

Late-Night Outdoor Soak Under Stars

One final yukata walk to your favorite bath. Snow falling on the canal. Lantern reflections. The town quiets after 10 PM — just you, the willows, and the sound of geta on stone.

Only here

Tokyo

Dec 26 (Sat) · 1 Night Transit · Sushi Reset Before Senjyuan
3 NIGHTS

Hoshinoya Tokyo

The most authentically Japanese luxury hotel in Tokyo. 17-floor vertical ryokan in Otemachi (financial district). 84 rooms across 14 floors, 6 per floor. All-tatami floors, woodblock keys, yukata-clad guests. Rooftop natural onsen pumped from 1,500m underground — the only proper onsen in central Tokyo. Each floor has its own ochanoma (traditional lounge) with snacks, tea, mochi 24/7. Hoshino Resorts.

“A real ryokan inside Tokyo. Rooftop natural hot spring with the city below. Genuinely impossible-in-NYC luxury.”

Bettei Senjyuan, Minakami

Day 9 · Sunday Dec 27
Mountain Forest Onsen & Joshu Beef
10:00 AM

Joetsu Shinkansen — Tokyo to Jomo-Kogen

~80 minutes from Tokyo Station, north into the Gunma mountains. Tunnels through the Mt. Tanigawa range. Then 25-min shuttle to the ryokan (the ryokan picks up at the station).

12:30 PM

Check In to Senjyuan

Greeted by name at the entrance. Welcome tea + Wagashi seasonal sweet. Tour of your room: a private outdoor stone onsen surrounded by forest, just outside your sliding door. The bath fills automatically and never empties — soak whenever you want.

Luxury
★ Your own private outdoor onsen, 24/7, all to yourself
1:30 PM — Light Lunch

Joshu Beef Soba in Minakami Town

Optional 10-min taxi to Minakami town for a regional specialty: handmade soba topped with seared Joshu beef (Gunma’s premium wagyu). Or stay at the ryokan for a light kaiseki lunch in your room.

Snack
3:00 PM — Sake Library

The Senjyuan Sake Tasting Room

The ryokan’s library lounge has 100+ Gunma sake, free for guests during designated hours. The staff sommelier guides you through a flight of regional brews you cannot get anywhere else.

Drink
4:30 PM — Long Soak

Private Onsen Sunset Bath

Back to your room. Snow on the cedars outside the bath. Steam rising. Glass of sake on the rim of the stone tub. This is the moment.

★ Snow falling on a private outdoor stone bath in deep mountain Japan
7:00 PM — Dinner

Multi-Course Joshu Beef Kaiseki

Served in a private dining room overlooking the inner garden (illuminated). 9-12 courses: amuse, otsukuri sashimi, simmered, charcoal-grilled Joshu beef, Mt. Tanigawa river fish, claypot rice, miso soup, seasonal dessert. Each course paired with a Gunma sake.

Fine Dining
10:00 PM — Night Bath

Stars Above the Onsen

Senjyuan is in the mountains, well outside city light pollution. On clear December nights the Milky Way stretches over the cedars. One last private soak before bed.
